How Do Blind People Get Around?

Blind people can have a harder time getting around, compared to those with sight. While they are usually able to do things on their own, sometimes help is required, from family, service animals, or sometimes even the kindness of strangers. In a world of technology and science, computers and products have been designed to improve the lives of those with visual impairments.

For example, an app known as “Seeing AI” uses a device's camera to recognize items and people in front of it, which is then used to notify the user of what they might be looking at. Links to download Seeing AI are below.

Hands-On Activity

Try It Yourself!

This is a short, interactive lesson and experiment to help you understand the effects of visual impairment on an person's life.


Materials

1. Some form of accessory, such as a hat, gloves, jacket, or scarf

2. Something to act as a blindfold, such as a blindfold or cloth

Make sure to read the entire step-by-step directions before blindfolding yourself!

1

Get Ready!

Layout the piece of clothing on a table in front of you

2

Experience!

With adult supervision or help, put on a blindfold.

3

Do It!

Start trying to put on your piece of clothing! If you have multiple, put them on one at a time. Be careful and keep awareness of your surroundings, and ensure adult supervision.

4

Can You Do It?

The goal is to experience what it's like to do everyday tasks without sight, such as dressing yourself. Try to put on your accessory without looking!

Reflection

Now we understand how hard it can be to do things while missing one of our most important senses. Talking to a partner, what do you think are some other things visually impaired people might struggle with?
