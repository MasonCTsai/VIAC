Service Animals and Canes

Blind people are able to get around by using a dog. The name for this dog is a guide dog, or seeing eye dogA dog used by blind individuals to guide them in public areas. They have a very important job. They help people know where bumps and holes may be. They can also help them cross the road.

This usually means that the service dog is looking for your help because something happened to their owner.

What do you think it means when a service dog approaches you without its owner? (Hover to reveal the answer!)

Another way those with visual impairments get around is using long, white canes.



The tip of the cane is used to scrape the floor. The bottom of the cane will make vibrations that the person can feel. This lets them know if there is a road or anything else nearby.


Blind people use their hearing a lot too. If they pay close attention, they can tell what teacher is coming up to them by just the jingle of their keys.

Hands-On Activity

Try It Yourself!

This is a short, interactive lesson and experiment to help you understand the how visual impairment affects the way a person travels.


Materials

1. A blindfold or cloth

2. A cane or long stick

Make sure to read the entire step-by-step directions before blindfolding yourself!

1

Get Ready!

Using a piece of cloth, securely blindfold yourself so that no light reaches your eyes, with adult supervision.

2

Experience!

With adult supervision, use the cane to travel from one side of the room to the other, navigating around obstacles. Take care not to hit anything too hard, and walk slowly to get a sense of your surroundings.

Reflection

With a partner, answer the following questions.

1. What was the first thing you noticed about moving around with a cane?

2. Did you have to be more careful?

3. How did you have to use your hearing or other senses?
