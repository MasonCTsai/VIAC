Hello,

Welcome to Lesson 1.2,

Try It Yourself

This is a short, interactive lesson and experiment to help you understand the effects of visual impairment on an individual's life.

Make sure to read the entire step-by-step directions before blindfolding yourself!

1

First, find a partner!

2

Using a blindfold, cover one person's eyes securely, so you can't see any light. If you don't have a blindfold, a cloth will do! Make sure you know where you are, in a safe environment, before blindfolding yourself.

Despite most cases of visual impairment not completely blocking out light, this activity simulates total darkness, as it more heavily emphasizes the differences of living with and without sight

3

Without being able to see, start to focus on the senses you do have access to. What do you smell, and what do you hear?

4

Have a partner place a random item on a table in front of you, in a random spot. Have the blindfolded partner attempt to locate and identify the object, using only their unobstructed senses, being their senses of smell, touch, or hearing.

5

Finally, discuss with your partner about the differences of living in and interacting with the world around you with and without a sense of sight to guide you. How did it feel to lose such a primary sense? Could you imagine living like this for the rest of your life?
