Not All Blindness is Equal

There are many different types of blindness. Those with visual impairment can develop it later in life, while others were born with it. Blindness that people are born with are called congenital (kuhn-JEH-nuh-tl) A type of visual impairment that is possessed from birth. There can be many different causes of blindness, and we'll talk more about them below.

Some of the images in this lesson may be triggering or graphic. If this is an issue, please refrain from continuing with this particular lesson.

1

Blindness can be caused by the loss of one or both of a person's eyes. This is known as anophthalmia(an-off-THAL-mee-ah) When a baby is born with one or both of its eyes missing.. Try pronouncing anophthalmia! (Hint: hover over the highlighted word for its pronunciation!)

2

Others may be born with both of their eyes, but things can go wrong with their growth, leading to the baby being born with eyes that are too small. This can happen to one or both eyes, leading to complete blindness for the eyes it affects.

3

Besides smaller eyes, poor eyesight can be caused by imperfections in the eye, especially the irisThe colored ring around the black dot on your eyes. This can cause complete blindness in worse cases.

4

CataractsCloudy, gray films within the eyes that can block vision are cloudy, gray films that develop on the inside of the eyeball, leading to a loss of vision. They are caused by a variety of reasons, including old age, or a person can be born with them. These can become worse and worse, where early stages do not cause total blindness. Over time, though, they can damage the eye enough to cause complete blindness.

5

Age-related macular degeneration (AMD)Also called AMD, it is when the macula of your eye worsens as you age and grow older. is when the maculaThe part of the eye that accepts and allows you to process light of your eye decays as you grow older. The macula is the part at the back of your eye that actually receives and processes light, allowing you to see. As you grow older, the macula can be damaged, leading to a loss of vision, though usually not completely. Though AMD cannot be seen from the outside, images like the one below will sometimes show a small spots on the eye. Try to see the difference in the images below!

6

Diabetic retinopathy(DIE-uh-beh-tick reh-ten-OP-uh-thee) The loss of vision because of diabetes is when you lose your vision due to diabetes. DiabetesA condition that causes a person to not have enough sugar in their blood, leading to loss of energy. is when you have naturally low amounts of sugar in your blood, meaning your cells aren't able to get the energy they need. This causes damage to the eye, eventually leading to blindness.

7

Glaucoma(gla-oh-KOH-muh) A condition that increases the pressure in the eyeball, leading to a loss of sight is when the nerve that connects your eye to your brain is damaged over a lot of time due to a lot of pressure inside the eye. Can you pronounce glaucoma? The disease is incurable, and is a common cause of blindness, leading to loss of vision, or even complete blindness. It can be congenital (remember, that means it was developed at birth!), or be gotten later in life. Visually, it is hard to see the effects of glaucoma, though it can sometimes cause redness in the eyes.
